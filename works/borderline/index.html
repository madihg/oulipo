Borderline

Dec '24 - Jan '25
Counterpulse AIR, San Francisco, CA
Live site ↗

Borderline is a speculative narrative performance set in a fictional “New Beirut,” where a mysterious red line appears across the city. The work unfolds through a layered score combining HTML poetry, news broadcasts, family stories, and border interrogations. It weaves movement, memory, and testimony to explore how migration reshapes the body and language.

The performance features an Arabic zajal refrain that returns across timelines, creating a sonic thread through the piece’s different temporal layers. Characters inhabit multiple roles: border agent, mother, informant, refugee. The performer’s body becomes a contested territory, oscillating between tenderness and surveillance.

In the final act, Borderline opens into a collective digital memory atlas where audience members share personal “red lines” - locations of home, belonging, loss. These submissions form a growing map: part cartography, part confessional, part prayer. The map continues to grow after the performance ends.

Borderline was developed during a Counterpulse Artist Residency in San Francisco and performed December 2024 through January 2025. It draws on the real history of Beirut’s Green Line, the demarcation that split the city during the civil war, and transposes it onto speculative futures where borders multiply, dissolve, and haunt.
