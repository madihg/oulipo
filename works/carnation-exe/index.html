Carnation.exe

May '25
European Artist Program, Paris, Barcelona
Live site ↗

Carnation.exe is Part I of the Singulars series, an ongoing artistic experiment in human-machine poetic confrontation. It stages a daily duel between a human poet and a fine-tuned language model trained exclusively on poetry. Inspired by the historic AlphaGo vs. Lee Sedol match, where a machine defeated a master Go player, the work proposes that creativity, like strategy, emerges through confrontation rather than isolation.

Each day, a theme is drawn. Both the poet and the model write a poem in response. Both are printed, placed side by side, anonymized, and displayed for the audience. Participants place small pink carnation dots beneath the poem they prefer. Votes determine which poem enters the next round of training data. The machine learns from the human’s losses. The human learns from the machine’s surprising coherence.

Pink carnations traditionally symbolize remembrance. Here they become instruments of aesthetic judgment, tiny gestures of attention that shape the future of a language model. Each vote is a form of curation. Each dot, a mark of care. The accumulated data becomes the training set for the next iteration, a feedback loop between taste and technology.

The work was first performed during the European Artist Program across Paris and Barcelona in May 2025. It continued to evolve across subsequent Singulars installments, with Versus.exe and Reinforcement.exe expanding the structure into longer, deeper confrontations.
