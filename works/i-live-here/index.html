I Live Here

Apr '25
Counterpulse AIR, San Francisco, CA
Live site ↗

I Live Here is a ritual descent - a katabasis - woven between Beirut and San Francisco’s Tenderloin. The piece explores belonging to places “built on bones”: BO18, a nightclub constructed atop the Karantina massacre site in Beirut, and The Hamilton, a residential hotel overlooking pandemic-era urban collapse in San Francisco.

The performance interrogates how hope curdles into fatalism, fatalism into myth. It is part personal memoir, part speculative geography, part séance. The performer moves between languages and cities, layering Arabic and English, memory and invention, testimony and song. A live digital interface accompanies the performance, displaying text fragments, maps, and visual echoes of both cities.

Developed during the CounterPulse Artist-in-Residence program in San Francisco, I Live Here was performed as part of the We-Topia Gala in April 2025. The piece concludes with an audience invitation: “downstairs, for tea, for testimony, for shared ground” - a gesture that transforms the theater into a living room, the performance into a gathering, the audience into neighbors.
