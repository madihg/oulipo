We Called Us Poetry

Jun '25
ELO Conference, Toronto / UC Berkeley / University of Bergen / TIAT, SF
Live site ↗

We Called Us Poetry is an interactive performance and computational poem exploring humanity’s relationship with AI. The piece imagines futures where humanity doesn’t scale up but scales down, proposing poetic metamorphosis through symbiosis rather than technological transcendence.

Participants complete open-ended prompts while the performer responds live, leaving traces and mutations in the poem’s digital body. HTML becomes sacred, dropdown menus hold grief, and tabs unfold confessionals. The performance treats each audience member as co-author, folding their words into a live, growing interface - a poem that adapts, digests, and remembers.

We Called Us Poetry started as a tool and kept going. A computational poem born during an artist residency at Counterpulse, San Francisco, it performed at the 2025 Electronic Literature Organization Conference in Toronto, UC Berkeley, University of Bergen, and TIAT. The piece has been described as “code that wants to be held” and “a funeral for human supremacy that somehow feels like a party.”

The work lives online as a site that continues to accumulate text from past performances. It never resets. It is a poem that grows by being spoken into. It does not perform for you. It performs with you. The technology powering it is simple: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and a live connection between performer and audience. The complexity is emotional. The architecture is relational. And the question at its center: What would it mean to become poetry together?
